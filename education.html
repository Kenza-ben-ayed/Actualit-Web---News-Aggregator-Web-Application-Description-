nTunisia-Ministry of Education: postponement of exams in public and private schools

Nadia Ayari, director general of primary education with the Ministry of Education stated today that a circular will soon be published to declare the postponement of first-quarter exams in public and private schools. She clarified that only the written tests of the first quarter will be maintained after the winter holidays. According to her, this will permit primary learners in public schools, affected by the strike of temporary teachers, to take advantage of 3 weeks of additional teaching.

From April 10 to 19: 126,210 students pass the sports baccalaureate

Quelque 126210 élèves passent cette année les épreuves du bac sport du 10 au 19 avril en cours. Ils sont répartis sur 255 centres à travers le pays. « Toutes les mesures nécessaires ont été prises pour assurer le bon déroulement des épreuves du bac sport dans tous les centres à travers le pays », a assuré lundi le ministre de l’éducation, Mohamed Ali Boughdiri. Assistant au démarrage des épreuves du bac sport au centre national d’athlétisme de Radès, le ministre a déclaré aux médias que le département est en train de se préparer activement à tous les examens et concours nationaux afin qu’ils aient lieu dans les délais et dans de bonnes conditions.

Secondary: the signing of an agreement with the Ministry of Education denied by the union

The Deputy Secretary General of the General Federation of Secondary Education, Nabil Hamrouni, said on Thursday that the information circulating concerning the signing of an agreement with the Ministry of Education are only rumours, stressing that the Negotiations between the two parties are continuing. He added, in a statement to TAP, that no progress has been made except for the staggered recruitment of substitute teachers.

Indians keen on learning courses for career growth: Pearson Skills Outlook report

21st April, 88% of workers in India, expect to continue learning and training throughout their career to stay relevant and attractive to employers, a new Pearson Skills Outlook report themed ‘Employee View’ has revealed. The report, second in the Skills Outlook series, highlights the shift in the attitude of the workforce towards upskilling, as they expect and explore learning and development, for their entire lifetime. A majority of the respondents who participated in the survey for the report, believe that learning a new language, specifically English, would help them advance in their careers.

A new perspective on learning: Qatar's 2030 vision for child education

Qatar has a vision for the coming years: to transform Qatar “into an advanced country by 2030, capable of sustaining its own development and providing for a high standard of living for all of its people for generations to come.” At its core, are four pillars; human development, economic development, social development, and environmental development, all aimed at driving the country forwards.
